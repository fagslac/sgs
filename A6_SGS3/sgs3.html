    3 - The Royal Cock Pit

    The walk between the Athenaeum club and Downing Street was eerily quiet. Chadt and Tian strolled down Horse Guards Road, St James's Park spreading its sickly foliage to their right. The war had depleted its population. The cold silence, deep as a cemetery, was only interrupted by the occasional lament of a duck.

    Chadt could almost forget it was not real. He could not take his eyes off the strange creature that stood out more than him in this world of steel and soot. His mind was still swamped by questions that wouldn't stop pouring.

    "If you're from the future, do you know everything that will happen?"

    Tian's feet hovered slightly above the ground. The angel's lean legs went slack. The body kept sliding along.

    "I can search our archives, but... they're rather empty. Most historical records were lost during the Singularity. That's precisely why we're doing this."

    Chadt's gaze went back to the envelope in his hand.

    For a fraction of a second, everything was different. The pond in the park simmered with thick blood. It covered the grass and climbed up on white stone buildings. A cold, metallic smell filled his lungs. Chadt's head was spinning.

    In the middle of the crimson ocean, a massive black cube shook the air with a deep throb.

    The image lasted for a second, then it was gone, replaced by a building that Chadt recognized from the news. The beating heart of British power. 10, Downing Street.

    The iconic obsidian door was crowned by an iridescent lantern. On either side, fences sliced the cold air into solemn strips. Chadt shrank under the building's oppressive aura.

    "It's supposed to be yellow," Tian interjected.

    Chadt blinked. Turned. The angel's wavy hair framed a pale, grinning face.

    "Everybody forgot about it. The smog turned it black, then they kept it that way."

    "Aren't you scared? pushed Chadt in disbelief. Isn't this the best protected place in the world?"

    Tian paused before answering.

    "Maybe in the Real Plane. But it's also very famous, which means we know it really well. Everything always leads back here. The Royal Cock Pit is basically a rite of passage for every student of my department."

    "The Royal Cockpit? asked Chadt with a sneer. Do they call it that because it's where they pilot the country?"

    "No, we call it that because it was a pit with actual poultry. Henry VIII built an arena for cockfighting right there, with guest lodgings next to it."

    Tian gestured towards the right. A trail of light followed.

    "It became a theater for a while, and then the seat of government. The cock fighting never really stopped though."

    Tian snickered. Chadt didn't. The angel studied the boy with inquisitive eyes.

    "Did I do humor wrong?"

    "I'm a little distracted because you're sending me into the last dungeon of the British empire," snapped Chadt.

    "Oh please," replied the angel with clearly unearned confidence. "This is barely the starting zone."

    The wind howled with the screams of millions. Chadt shuddered. The sun reflecting off the overly polished walls blinded him.

    "So how do we get in?"

    Tian repeated exactly the graceful pointing gesture.

    "We can enter through number 11. It was condemned when they joined the houses, but here a door is a door."

    "What about the security? Is there an army of steampunk guards?"

    "Actually, during most of its existence this place was relatively poorly protected. There are of course the fellows at the door."

    Tian gestured towards two black shapes that Chadt had not noticed. The guards seemed to have sedentarized into the wall. Their proportions far exceeded those of normal humans, and their skin resembled stone. They did not seem to breathe.

    "But since we'll be bypassing them, we'll only see staff and ministers, much too busy with their own chores and their own selves to pay us any mind."

    "You're blue and sparkling," observed Chadt dubitative.

    "And you really underestimate how self-involved british gentlemen can get. Still, you're right to be cautious."

    Tian seemed absent for a while. Then the colors of both of their clothes faded to dim, dark shade of grey. They looked almost transparent.

    "I weakened our signal, that should be enough," Tian explained, marching ahead. "Of course, the best way to not get in trouble is to minimize our exposure time. Let's go, they might be signing the letter already."

    "Wait!"

    Chadt tried to grab his companion by the arm, but his hand passed through. Tian stopped anyway.

    "What happens after the ritual? I mean, what's our way out?"

    Tian seemed surprised by the question

    "I figured you would just wake up."

    Chadt did not know what to say to that. He supposed this was a valid strategy.

    He ran after Tian, who was already pushing the much smaller, much more innocuous door numbered 11. Chadt stared apprehensively as the stone guardians, but they did not move an inch. They might have stood like this for centuries.
