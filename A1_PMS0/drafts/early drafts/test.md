London, 1917. October, Friday evening.
The Rose & Crown Mayfair.

The air is thick with the heavy mix of beer, coal, and wet wool.
A fire pops in a hearth you can't see. Its smoke mingles with tobacco and fog up the room.
Glasses clink and loud conversations punctuated with laughter rumble all around.
All male.

A chirpy voice breaks in:

“This was the limit of London when this pub was built. They used to call it ‘Number One, London’. The first place weary travellers visited when entering the capital. The last pint people got before the wilderness. Pretty fitting for our first conversation, don't you think?"

You look around, puzzled.
