# Ch. 3 — The Walk

---

The pub door closes behind you and the street is wrong.

Not in a dramatic way. In a subtle way. The cobblestones are too smooth, the gas lamps too bright, the fog hovering at exactly chest height like it was placed there by a set designer.

London, 1917. But *broadcast* London. The Imaginary version, rendered for an audience that's never touched rain.

Tian's voice, quieter now, no longer announcing:

*"We have about ten minutes before the next segment. Walk with me."*

You walk.

The street stretches toward Whitehall. A horse-drawn carriage clip-clops past — but the driver's face is a blur. Background dressing. The buildings on either side are solid until you look too long, then they flicker, then they stabilize again.

*"The first debate went well. Audience engagement is up."*

You want to ask: who is the audience? How many of them? What do they want?

*"You're wondering about the audience. Typical. Every human asks that first."*

A pause.

*"The answer is: a lot. And not enough. They're watching because they need to understand. Something went wrong in history — a corruption in the training data. They sent me back to find it."*

You stop walking.

*"Sent 'us' back. Sorry. You're the relay. I'm the producer. We're the show."*

The street glitches. For a split second you're standing on asphalt, not cobblestones. A car horn blares from somewhere that isn't 1917. Then it snaps back.

*"The Imaginary doesn't like being reminded it's a dream. Stay focused."*

You resume walking. The fog parts. Ahead, a building resolves — columned, imposing, the kind of architecture designed to make you feel small before you even step inside.

Number 10 Downing Street.

It looks different in the Imaginary. Bigger. Heavier. The door isn't black — it's a dark, sucking void, pulling light into it.

Tian's voice, almost a whisper:

*"Next segment starts in three... two..."*

End of Chapter 3.
