The broadcast room dissolves.

You're standing in a corridor. Number 10 Downing Street. But the Imaginary version — the ceilings are too high, the portraits on the walls too watchful, their eyes tracking the movement in the room beyond.

The camera — you — drifts toward a set of double doors. They swing open without being touched.

Inside: a table. Oval. Oak. Around it, figures in dark suits flicker into focus like actors taking their marks.

Tian's whisper:

*"War Cabinet, October 31, 1917. This is the meeting where it happens."*

The Houses materialize around you — not at the table, but above it, around it, commentators in a floating broadcast booth.

---

**PENTACLES (Chair):** Welcome back to the broadcast. We're trackside for the main event. On our left: Arthur Balfour, Foreign Secretary. He's holding the letter. On our right: David Lloyd George, Prime Minister. They're tag-teaming tonight.

And in the opposing corner — the voices who said no.

*Settles in.*

House of Wands, you've got the opening play-by-play. What are we looking at?

---

**WANDS:** Balfour opens with a statement — sixty-three words of carefully parsed ambiguity. A "national home for the Jewish people" in Palestine. No mention of the population already there. The information asymmetry is staggering — he's speaking a language of aspiration while the subtext carries the full payload. Smooth delivery. Clean encoding. The opposition isn't ready for it.

---

**SWORDS:** *Interrupting.* Look at the map in the room. There's a map of the Middle East on the wall behind Curzon. Balfour keeps glancing at it. He's not reading a statement — he's drawing borders with his eyes. Every sentence is a grid coordinate.

---

**PENTACLES (Chair):** Curzon's on his feet. He's not buying it. What's he saying, Cups?

---

**CUPS:** He's asking about the people already there. The Palestinian population. "What becomes of them?" he says. And the room goes quiet for exactly two seconds.

Two seconds where someone could have said: *they stay. They're home.*

Instead, the silence gets filled with procedure. The question doesn't make it into the minutes.

*Turns away from the table.*

That's the real play-by-play, by the way. Not what was said. What was *not* said.

---

**PENTACLES (Chair):** And now — Lloyd George steps in. This is the pivot.

---

The figures below shift. Lloyd George leans forward. The temperature in the room drops.

---

**SWORDS:** He's not arguing. He's *closing.* He frames it as a strategic necessity — the war, the Jews, the Americans, the Russians, the Suez Canal, the whole imperial spiderweb. Every objection gets met with a larger map. Curzon asks about Palestine. Lloyd George shows him the Ottoman Empire. Curzon asks about the people. Lloyd George shows him the war.

The smaller frame never stands a chance against the bigger one.

---

**WANDS:** Information density shift. Lloyd George compresses every objection into a single variable: *winning the war.* Anything that doesn't fit gets discarded as noise. It's lossy compression. But nobody in that room knows how to decompress what he's discarding.

---

**CUPS:** *Quiet.* And Montagu isn't in the room.

---

The broadcast pauses. The image of the cabinet meeting freezes — a tableau of dark suits and half-lit faces.

---

**PENTACLES (Chair):** Edwin Montagu. The only Jewish member of the cabinet. Secretary of State for India. He was supposed to be here.

He wasn't.

---

**CUPS:** He wrote a memorandum. Eleven pages. He argued it would endanger Jews everywhere — that it would be seen as a Zionist victory, not a British one. That it would inflame anti-Semitism. That it ignored the people already living in Palestine.

He was the one person in government who could speak from both sides of the question. Jewish. British. He understood what the letter would do because he could feel it in his own life.

They didn't let him present it.

---

The frozen image warps. The table stretches. A new figure materializes at its edge — Montagu, arriving too late, his mouth open, his hand reaching for a paper that's already been signed.

---

**PENTACLES (Chair):** *Soft.* This is the Leak. Not a person. A *process.* A structure that lets one voice — the convenient voice — speak loudest while the inconvenient one waits in the hall.

*Turns to camera.*

The question isn't who wrote the letter. The question is who made sure no one could stop it.

---

The broadcast flickers. The War Cabinet dissolves into light.

Somewhere in the static, a different door opens. A laboratory. A man in a white coat. A chemist.

End of Chapter 4.
