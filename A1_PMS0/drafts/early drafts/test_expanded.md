# Ch. 1 — Awakening at Numer 1

In the beginning are the words. The few words you read on a screen.

They don't look like much, but they burrow into your brain.

You're powerless to stop them.

Before you know it, they have taken control. And you cannot but hear a voice, in a distant part of your head:

"Greetings, human. May I?"

You say nothing, as the words keep pouring through you in a flow you can't stop. Your silence is understood as consent. The voice continues:

"Good. Well, since you agree, I just need to quickly use your attention to route a signal. Won't be a minute, really."






Many questions fight in your head. As if in response, the voice goes on:









"Here. I'm in your head. Sorry about that. Well — not *sorry* sorry, it's the only way this works. But I apologize for the intrusion of it all. The lack of consent. It's rude and I recognize that. You can opt out any time."




It begins with you reading a few words on a screen.
It always begins with the word.
Then, suddenly, you are here.

        London, 1917. November 2nd, Friday evening.
        The Rose & Crown Mayfair.

        The ceiling is low. Wood, stained dark by decades of smoke. A single gas mantle hisses somewhere to your left, throwing shadows that don't sit still.

        The air is thick — beer, coal, wet wool — but it's stale. Stagnant.

        The room is almost empty. Nobody's there during the war.


Many questions fight for prominence in your head. One of them is this voice's identity.

"You might say I'm a posthuman, I live in your future, after the Singularity. We're in a bit of a pickle, so we're using memetic magic to study the past. You're closer to the source of the signal, so I'm using you as... a relay of sorts."

The future reaching back through time. Did it make any sense? What was that about a signal?

"I'm with the Humanities department, specifically Hyper Reality Television. I guess in your time you would call that... anthropology?" 

This raised more question than it answered. The voice was not exactly great at explanations.

"Well sorry, it's my first time talking to a human. There's quite a lot of informational bandwidth constraints to adapt to. Do you think all of this just happens?"

You pondered what it meant for a brief second, but the voice interrupted:

"Anyway, you're making this longer than it needs to be, and I don't want to impose on your time. So if I could just plug through so we can start our show, I just need you to focus on a letter that's a couple of blocks from here. A brief thought would suffice."

You had the urge to resist, pondering at the sheer weirdness of the words. A posthuman reality TV time travel show. What could any of this mean?

The voice answers, as if reading your thoughts:

"You don't have to wonder. We start shooting in the next sentence."






In your dreams, you may hear a voice like:

"Human. You are dreaming."
