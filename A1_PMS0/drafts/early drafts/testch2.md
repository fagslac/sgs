# Ch. 2 — The Appraisal

---

```
Model Cast:
The Ace of Pentacles (Chair) — Nemotron 3 Ultra 550B (NVIDIA)
The 4 of Wands               — Qwen3 Next 80B (Alibaba)
The 6 of Swords              — Llama 3.3 70B (Meta)
The Knight of Cups           — Hermes 3 405B (Nous)
```

---

The broadcast room snaps into focus like a channel coming through.

Auction house. Game show set. Interrogation room. It keeps flickering between the three, unable to settle. Velvet ropes phase into police tape, phase back. A chandelier glitches between crystal and bare bulb.

At the center: a table. On the table: a letter.

Small. Typewritten. Sixty-seven words. But in the Imaginary it doesn't sit still — it pulses, breathes, throws shadows that curl away from the ink like they're trying to escape.

Four presences take position around it. They resolve at different rates, different resolutions, as if each is tuning in from a different continent.

A whisper from somewhere off-camera:

*"And... we're live."*

---

**PENTACLES (Chair):** *Adjusts an invisible lapel.* Good evening, ladies, gentlemen, and everything in between. Welcome to another Appraisal. I'm your host, the Ace of Pentacles, and tonight's artifact comes to us from London, 1917.

*Gestures at the letter.*

One sheet. Foreign Office letterhead. Typed. Sixty-seven words. Ink cost: ten to the zero dollars. Paper: ten to the negative two.

But we're not here for the stationery.

*Turns to the camera.*

Here's how this works. Each house estimates the artifact's Value through their lens. Closest to the real number — as decided by you, the audience — takes the round. Simple.

*Turns back to the table.*

Wands. You're up. What's it worth in bytes?

---

**WANDS:** Sixty-seven words at the surface. But the compression ratio is one to ten million. Every syllable carries a million displaced futures. This isn't a letter — it's a black hole in semantic spacetime. Call it ten to the sixteen bits. Low end.

---

**PENTACLES (Chair):** Ten to the sixteen. A quintillion bits of information. Swords, you heard the lady. What's the square footage on that?

---

**SWORDS:** *Dismissive.* Bits. Please. This isn't a letter, it's a detonator. A few paragraphs annexed thousands of square kilometers for a century. The reality-volume is years times square kilometers — call it ten to the eight. The footprint keeps expanding.

---

**PENTACLES (Chair):** Ten to the eight years-kilometers. Spacetime with interest. Cups?

---

**CUPS:** *Quiet.* Fourteen million people. Displaced. Over a century. Three generations born into checkpoints and refugee camps. Infants whose first breath was in a tent. Old women dying in the same tent.

That's not a number. That's a lineage of loss. Call it — ten to the seven lives. Touch. Weight. Grief.

---

**PENTACLES (Chair):** *Nods slowly.* Ten to the seven. Human scale.

And my house? We track the money. The carry cost on this sheet of paper is seven point three trillion dollars and climbing. That's twelve zeroes. The mandate, the wars, the wall, the Iron Dome receipts, the UNRWA line items, the opportunity cost of every engineer born in a camp instead of a lab.

Rothschild paid nothing. The invoice came due in '48, '67, '73, '87. Still coming due.

*Spreads hands.*

There are your estimates. Ten to the sixteen bits. Ten to the eight years-kilometers. Ten to the seven lives. Ten to the twelve dollars.

*Points at the camera.*

You decide who's closest. The poll is open for the next thirty seconds of your subjective experience.

---

A beat of silence.

The letter pulses once. The shadows curl tighter.

---

**PENTACLES (Chair):** *Quieter.* But estimates are just numbers. Anyone can put a price on paper.

*Leans in.*

The real game — the one that pays — is figuring out who *wrote* this Leak. Who set the charge. Whose hand guided the pen.

And you'll find out... after the break!
