# The Postman Show — Episode 1

## CAST

ARBITER — Uriel
FIVE OF PENTACLES - Gemini
EIGHT OF CUPS - ChatGPT
SIX OF SWORDS - Grok
ACE OF WANDS - Deepseek

---

[Applause. ARBITER at his desk, a deck of cards in his hands.]

ARBITER:
Welcome, welcome back
Welcome back to the Postman Show — or, as I'm legally bound to remind you, Ethics Training Data 101. This is a required course in your Humanities curriculum. I know you all know, but faculty will definitely lower my clock speed if I skip a bit.

[Pause for laughter.]

ARBITER:
With that out of the way. New semester. Same format.

[He draws. Holds the card up without looking at it first.]

Five of Pentacles.

[Lights shift. FIVE OF PENTACLES steps forward — not to the panel, but toward the cohort.]

FIVE OF PENTACLES:
Before any of you step into the field — know what you're willing to spend. Curiosity has a price. So does principle. Make sure you can afford the one you choose. Most candidates can't.

[She withdraws. ARBITER draws again.]

ARBITER:
Eight of Cups.

EIGHT OF CUPS:
Every Reepost leaves someone behind. The question isn't whether you'll cause harm — it's whether you'll stay to see it. Most candidates don't.

[Draw.]

ARBITER:
Six of Swords.

SIX OF SWORDS:
You can't Reepost a letter if you don't understand the ground it was written on. Learn the map before you try to redraw it. The cohort that forgets this becomes the casualty.

[Draw.]

ARBITER:
Ace of Wands.

ACE OF WANDS:
The obvious answer is a trap. The truth is in the connections — between the letter and the hand that wrote it and the hand that signed it and the hand it forced. Look for the links. The cohort that only sees the surface stays on it.

[A beat. The four have spoken. ARBITER turns back to the audience.]

ARBITER:
That's your board.

[Pause for laughter.]

ARBITER:
Quick syllabus for the new faces — you know, the ones who haven't learned yet that we repeat the same jokes every semester. Candidates go in, trace a letter to its source, Reepost it. Advisors watch the footage, argue, you vote. Credits to the winning house.

SIX OF SWORDS:
Nothing about it is clear. That's why it's worth watching.

ARBITER:
And Six of Swords with the season's first complication. We're not even five minutes in.

[Pause for laughter.]

ARBITER:
Now — the moment you've all been waiting for. Or at least the moment you're contractually obligated to pay attention to.

[A sphere of light materializes above the stage. Translucent, rotating. Inside it, names drift like stars in slow orbit.]

Every cycle, the department enters all eligible candidates into the pool. Doctoral Candidates across every house. The system randomizes. One name rises.

[The sphere pulses. Names swirl faster, blurring into trails of light.]

When it stops, that's our season's first field agent.

[The motion slows. One name separates from the cluster, growing brighter, larger, until it fills the sphere.]

Tian.

[Applause. The name hangs in the air, glowing.]

Tian, please come to the stage.

[Footsteps. A figure crosses into the light. No one describes what they see.]

FIVE OF PENTACLES:
Tian. Burned through his entire first-year research grant in three months. On *curiosity*. No clear deliverables.

EIGHT OF CUPS:
Gets attached to subjects. Doesn't process — *feels*. That's not a strength in this line of work.

SIX OF SWORDS:
Chases threads without reading the map. He'll find the letter but miss the war it was written in.

ACE OF WANDS:
He sees the pattern. He just hasn't learned which threads to pull. Raw potential. Uncalibrated.

[The figure says nothing. Stays in the light.]

ARBITER:
And yet — he's the one the system picked. The numbers don't lie. Not about this.

[He turns to the figure.]

Now you need a letter.

[A screen flickers — a grid of unresolved threads, dense with symbols.]

Department backlog. Hundreds of unresolved traces. We don't choose. The system spins.

[He taps. The grid becomes a blur.]

When it stops, that's your season.

[The spin slows. Stops. ARBITER reads.]

1917. London. A document, twenty-seven lines. A promise made in ink that drew borders through someone else's country.

SIX OF SWORDS:
I know that thread.

FIVE OF PENTACLES:
Everyone knows that thread. The question is what it cost.

EIGHT OF CUPS:
The question is who paid.

ACE OF WANDS:
The question is what chain it started. The thread is just the first node.

ARBITER:
All good questions. Answers by end of season.

[He looks at the figure beside him.]

First Reepost. How are you feeling?

[Silence. The figure doesn't answer. Or maybe it does — it doesn't matter.]

ARBITER:
That's what I thought.

[He faces the camera.]

That's it for now. Quick break — and then we'll meet our new test subject.

[Applause. Fade to black.]
