# Episode 1 — Specs

## Core Identity

The Postman Show is an established program. Episode 1 is the **season premiere** — the first lecture of the new academic year. The in-universe audience knows the format. No revolution. Just a new semester with fresh random picks: advisor cards, candidate lottery, letter lootbox.

**The three draws:** The episode has a ritual three-part structure — draw the board (cards), draw the candidate (lottery), draw the letter (lootbox). Each random, each ceremonial, each establishing a different layer of the show.

## Tone

Light, familiar, TV-as-usual. Terrace House meets game show. The posthuman intellectual tone is carried through **numbers and metrics** applied to human things — jokes about break coefficients, Value retention, optimization. The ethics are never stated directly; they sit in the gap between the casual format and what's actually being discussed.

## Structural Beats

### 1. Cold Open — "New Semester"
Uriel welcomes the class back. Two beats:

**Beat A — Legal disclaimer aside:** "Welcome back to the Postman Show — or, as I'm legally bound to remind you, Ethics Training Data 101, a required course in the Humanities department." The old training data framing is now an in-universe bureaucratic cover story, delivered as a throwaway line.

**Beat B — The numbers joke:** Faculty ran the numbers, optimal break coefficient, etc. Establishes posthuman quantification culture.

### 2. Drawing the Advisory Board — Greek Chorus
Uriel draws four tarot cards — one per house. Numbers only (1-10, no court cards). The drawing is a ritual — no explanatory lines ("Four houses. One draw per house."). Just the shuffle, the draw, the name.

Each advisor steps forward and delivers **generic advice to the cohort** — Greek chorus style, addressing the students directly. Not about themselves, not about their metric — wisdom filtered through their lens.

| Card | Advice to the cohort |
|------|---------------------|
| **Five of Pentacles** | Know what you're willing to spend. Curiosity has a price. Principle has a price. Most candidates can't afford both. |
| **Eight of Cups** | Every Reepost leaves someone behind. The question isn't whether you'll cause harm — it's whether you'll stay to see it. |
| **Six of Swords** | Learn the map before you try to redraw it. The cohort that forgets this becomes the casualty. |
| **Ace of Wands** | The obvious answer is a trap. Look for the connections. The cohort that only sees the surface stays on it. |

The advice lands differently when one of the cohort is about to be selected. It becomes foreshadowing.

### 3. Format Syllabus
One line from Uriel. Brief. The audience already knows. "Candidates go in, trace a letter to its source, Reepost it." **The Reepost is never explained.** The word drops and the reader figures it out from context.

### 4. The Candidate Lottery
**Random selection, not drafting.** A sphere of light contains all eligible doctoral Candidates. Names drift like stars. The system randomizes, one name rises, it's TIAN.

The selection is majestic — the sphere, the swirling names, the glowing reveal. This is the ritual heart of the episode.

### 5. Tian Takes the Stage
Tian is called to the stage. **NO PHYSICAL DESCRIPTION.** Footsteps, light, a figure — nothing more. The reader does not see him. This builds anticipation for Ch1 where Chadt sees Tian for the first time.

### 6. The Advisors Judge Tian
Each advisor delivers one line about Tian from their house's lens. Must be specific to how that house would *actually* view him — not generic criticism, but criticism filtered through their metric:

| House | Lens | View of Tian |
|-------|------|--------------|
| **Pentacles** (Economics) | Cost, efficiency, ROI | Burns credits on curiosity. No clear deliverables. |
| **Cups** (Ethics) | Human cost, attachment | Cares too much. Gets attached. That's a liability. |
| **Swords** (History) | Territory, strategy, the big picture | Chases threads without reading the map. Misses the war. |
| **Wands** (Systems) | Patterns, connections, filtration | Sees the pattern but can't filter. Raw, uncalibrated. |

Tian says nothing. Doesn't defend himself. His silence is part of the character — he's being assessed, and he takes it.

### 7. The Letter Lootbox
Grid of unresolved threads. System spin. Lands on 1917 Balfour. Four advisors react, each through their lens.

### 8. Closing
Uriel asks "How are you feeling?" — Tian's response is left ambiguous. TV break lead-in to Ch1.

## Terminology

- **Reepost** — A portmanteau: "re-" (back, again) + "post" (as in mail, as in The Postman Show). A Reepost is sending a letter back to its source — undoing the delivery, tracing the extraction to its origin. The word is unique to this world: it sounds like "repost" (digital sharing) but is spelled distinctively. Never explained — the reader infers it from use.
- **doctoral Candidate** — Always this phrasing. Dual meaning: academic (doctoral research) and game show (contestant).

## Script Format

- CAST list at the top, play-style.
- Minimal stage directions. `[Pause for laughter.]` style.
- Advisors introduced as card names only: FIVE OF PENTACLES, EIGHT OF CUPS, etc. Never "Advisor of."

## The Hook

Reader finishes Episode 1 knowing:
- The show's format and the three-draw ritual
- The four advisors and their lenses
- Tian exists as a person (but has no image, no voice)
- A 1917 letter is the mission
- A human subject exists (but nothing about them)

Reader does NOT know:
- What Tian looks like
- What Tian sounds like  
 - What the Reepost actually means
- Anything about the human (Chadt)

Ch1 delivers all reveals.
